Steven Jeggels Invitational

Celebrating Legacy, Creating Opportunities

The Steven Jeggels Invitational honors the remarkable legacy of Steven John Jeggels, a pioneering surfer who broke barriers and dedicated his life to nurturing young talent. This competition continues his vision of giving underprivileged children opportunities for a better life through surfing.

Steven John Jeggels - Pioneer, Champion, Mentor

Early Life & Passion

Born in Fairview, Gqeberha, Steven first discovered his love for skateboarding before transitioning to surfing at age 14. After matriculating from HSSS in 1983, he worked night shifts at "Our Times" newspaper to surf during the day.

Championship Glory

Steven became a three-time South African Surfing Union (SASU) champion, securing titles in 1987, 1988, and 1989. In 1990, he turned professional and began his ASP Tour in Europe.

Breaking Barriers

Steven made history as the first person of colour from Pellsrus, Jeffreys Bay, to surf professionally. His journey was supported by his brothers Owen and Paul, who believed in his dream.

Freedom Raiders Legacy

In 1994, Steven founded the "Freedom Raiders" Surf Academy Club to teach young children from Pellsrus how to surf, established shortly after Nelson Mandela became President.

The Mission

Kitchen Windows Heritage

For long, Kitchen Windows has been the birthplace where surfers of colour find belonging and freedom. This competition celebrates that heritage.

Future Champions

This event aims to help unearth some of the best surfers of the future, giving them opportunities to demonstrate their gift and passion.

Family & Community

Steven was a devoted father to Dane and Malia Jeggels, and a founding member of the Jeffreys Bay Surf Club in 1983.
